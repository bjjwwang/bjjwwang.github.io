Plato's foundational work on justice, the ideal state, and the nature of the human soul. The Allegory of the Cave alone makes it worth reading — a powerful metaphor for how we mistake shadows for reality. Two thousand years later, its questions about truth, power, and education remain remarkably relevant. The kind of book that rewards re-reading at different stages of life.
